About

Virtual Agora offers a unique platform to facilitate constructive dialogue among people of different views.

In the ancient Greek democracies, people met in marketplaces called agoras to discuss community issues and make collective decisions. Technology provides us with unprecedented easiness in communicating. Paradoxically, most of our problems result from our inability to talk with each other. We hope to change it at Virtual Agora. To accomplish that, we have adopted the following principles:

  1. The sky is the limit. There is no problem that we cannot fix by working together.
  2. We invite writers presenting opposing views to participate in the Socratic dialogue. Please write down your ideas and submit them for the relentless scrutiny by others, and be equally exacting in your polemics with others.
  3. We encourage polemics, which are the forgotten art of journalism. We can be ruthlessly critical of one’s views and respectful at the same time.
  4. We welcome responses to comments by readers, preferably in the form of a separate article.
  5. At Virtual Agora, the power of argument rules. The arguments of power — be it fame, money, political influence, or the ability to block critics on Medium — do not apply here.
  6. We work together to make our life better, on big political issues as much as on petty everyday annoyances.

In media and in politics it is often assumed that there is some wisdom in the voice of the majority. Prohibition is the best example that nonsense, even if agreed to by the majority and even if voted into law, is still just nonsense. Opposite than in media and politics, at Virtual Agora we do not try to find out what the majority thinks the truth is; we try to figure it out what the truth actually is.

The truth is often not what we wish it would be. When listening to our opponents, we often sigh at how good it would be to stick to facts and follow logic. They do the same when listening to our arguments. Unfortunately, we rarely talk to each other respectfully. Our inability to speak candidly makes our problems even worse, to the point that they seem unsolvable. We launched Virtual Agora with a conviction that we can change this damaging circle of political miscommunication.
